2.12 Roll-cake

Roll-cake, also known as Swiss roll, is a type of rolled-up cake containing some kind of filling. It is a soft cake that can be chopped up into pieces, making it a perfect addition to any fika. Roll-cakes come in many different forms; this recipe is for a roll with orange-filling.


Figure 6: Finished roll-cake with orange filling.

Ingredients

  • 3 eggs
  • 1.5 dl sugar
  • 0.75 dl potato-flour
  • 0.25 dl wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp baking-powder
 Orange-filling:
  • 2 gelatin leaves
  • 1 egg
  • 0.5 dl sugar
  • 1.5 dl cream
  • Grated peel of half an orange
  • 1.5 dl pressed orange juice

Instructions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 250 degrees; 200 degrees in fan-assisted oven mode.
  2. Whip the egg and sugar until it is puffy.
  3. Mix potato-flour, wheat flour and baking-powder. Turn it into the batter.
  4. Spread the batter on baking paper in a long pan, circa 30 x 40 cm.
  5. Bake in the middle of the oven for about 5 minutes.
  6. Overturn the cake onto sugared paper or a damp cloth. Pull away the baking paper and let the cake cool off.
  7. Put the gelatin leaves entirely in cold water.
  8. Mix the egg, sugar and cream in a pot. Let the mixture simmer on low temperature while whipping it until it thickens. The filling will divide itself if it boils.
  9. Wring out the gelatin leaves and put them into the filling. Stir until they melt.
  10. Add the grated orange peel and the orange juice. Let the filling cool off.
  11. Spread the orange filling on the cake and roll it together. Let the roll-cake cool off before it is cut.
