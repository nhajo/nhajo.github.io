Wide Potato Noodles

Wide potato noodles is a traditional dish in Chinese households, usually served with improvised vegetables.

Figure 20: Wide potato noodles with cucumber and celery.

Ingredients

Serves 1 person

  • Potato noodles for one
  • 50–80g improvised meat (minced preferred)
  • 1/4 cucumber
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 cm Welsh onion or 1/4 onion
  • Other improvised vegetables
  • (Recommended) 5–10g coriander

Seasoning:

  • Any oil or butter
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 small box chilli flakes
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp laoganma
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp vinegar

Instructions

  1. Chop the onion and garlic, slice the cucumber.
  2. Boil the potato noodles until nearly cooked. Rinse with cold water and set aside.
  3. Fry meat in oil. Once cooked, add chilli flakes, garlic, and onion. Fry for 30 seconds.
  4. Add 50ml water, oyster sauce, laoganma, soy sauce. Stir in noodles and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  5. Add sugar, salt, coriander, and sesame oil. Mix and serve.
